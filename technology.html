Technology Architecture

Five AI Disciplines. One Reasoning System.

AIMAILABS integrates multiple AI technologies into a unified reasoning architecture purpose-built for safety-critical engineering environments. Each technology addresses a specific layer of the integrity intelligence problem.

CV
Computer Vision

Anomaly detection across engineering disciplines directly from inspection images. Identifies defect type, location, extent, and morphology — whether corrosion on steel, coating failure on piping, degradation on cable trays, or damage to civil foundations.

KG
Knowledge Graphs

Multi-discipline engineering knowledge representation. Encodes standards, criteria, and asset relationships across structural, mechanical, piping, electrical, instrumentation, civil, and safety domains into a queryable reasoning system.

GNN
Graph Neural Networks

Asset relationship analysis across disciplines. Models how anomalies propagate through connected components — from structural members to piping networks to electrical systems — and affect system-level integrity.

NLP
Natural Language Processing

Technical report and work scope generation. Produces structured engineering documentation from assessment outputs.

M-AI
Multimodal AI

Combines inspection images, discipline-specific engineering rules, and asset data into a unified reasoning context for cross-discipline assessment and decision support.

How They Connect

From Raw Image to
Engineering Decision

Each technology layer operates on the output of the one before it. The result is a continuous reasoning pipeline that transforms a raw inspection photograph into a standards-aligned engineering assessment — with full traceability at every step.

No single AI model solves the integrity problem alone. The architecture is designed so that vision, knowledge, language, and graph reasoning operate as an integrated system — not as isolated point solutions.

The Problem

Offshore Integrity Still Runs
on Disconnected Workflows

The offshore structural integrity lifecycle — inspection, engineering assessment, and maintenance — still depends on manual workflows, fragmented tooling, and subjective engineering judgment applied inconsistently across assets and teams.

Inspection findings live in PDF reports. Engineering assessments exist in spreadsheets. Maintenance scopes are built from memory and institutional knowledge. None of these systems share context, and none of them learn.

01
Manual Defect Assessment

Inspectors photograph defects and classify severity based on experience. Two inspectors assessing the same finding may reach different conclusions. Reports take hours or days to compile, often only after returning to shore.

02
Disconnected Engineering Analysis

Inspection data flows into separate engineering systems — if it flows at all. Assessments against standards happen in isolation, with no traceability from the original finding to the engineering conclusion.

03
Reactive Maintenance Planning

Maintenance scopes are built manually after campaigns complete. Work scopes are assembled from scratch without structured linkage to the findings they address. Critical repairs compete with routine work for the same execution window.

04
No Shared Intelligence Layer

Inspection, engineering, and maintenance teams operate on different tools with different data formats. No unified system connects a photographed defect to a standards-based assessment to a repair work order — with full traceability.

Engineering analysis reports generated from inspection findings. Standards-referenced, fully traceable from the original defect photograph to the engineering conclusion. Audit-ready documentation produced in minutes, not weeks.

ANO
COREVA Anode
Cathodic Protection Trending

Track anode consumption across the asset portfolio. AI-driven depletion curves, remaining life estimates, and replacement scheduling — turning cathodic protection management into a data-driven operation.

Predictive Inspection Planning

Risk-based inspection planning powered by degradation trending and historical findings data. Identifies where the next critical defect is likely to develop before it reaches an actionable threshold.

COREVA Fitness
Fitness-for-Service Screening

When a defect exceeds inspection criteria, determine whether continued operation is justified. AI-assisted FFS screening with full calculation traceability — accept, repair, or monitor, with documented engineering basis.

COREVA Thickness
Wall Thickness Management

UT wall thickness management across the CML network. Historical trending, short-term and long-term corrosion rates, remaining life calculations, and next inspection date scheduling — systematically applied.

FTG

Cumulative fatigue damage tracking against S-N curves and joint classification per design codes. Remaining fatigue life estimates per member and joint — critical for ageing jacket structures and topsides.

Topsides Weight Management

Track cumulative weight changes and centre-of-gravity drift from structural modifications and equipment changes. Flag weight budget exceedances and link back to structural capacity assessments.

Technology Architecture

Five AI Disciplines. One Reasoning System.

AIMAILABS integrates multiple AI technologies into a unified reasoning architecture purpose-built for safety-critical engineering environments. Each technology addresses a specific layer of the integrity intelligence problem.

CV
Computer Vision

Structural anomaly detection directly from inspection images. Identifies defect type, location, extent, and morphology.

KG
Knowledge Graphs

Engineering knowledge representation. Encodes standards, criteria, and structural relationships into a queryable reasoning system.

GNN
Graph Neural Networks

Structural relationship analysis. Models how defects propagate through connected components and affect system-level integrity.

NLP
Natural Language Processing

Technical report and work scope generation. Produces structured engineering documentation from assessment outputs.

M-AI
Multimodal AI

Combines inspection images, engineering rules, and asset data into a unified reasoning context for cross-domain assessment.
